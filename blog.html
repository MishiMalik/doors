Choosing a Door for

Home Or Business

So you’ve decided that it’s time to upgrade your door. But what type of door is right for you and your home or business?

Choosing the right door can be a difficult decision with all the different types out there. Is a sliding glass door better than a hinged one? A steel security door better than wood? What even ARE bifold doors?

If these questions have been keeping you awake at night, fear not! We’re here to help.

From deciding on materials to choosing between styles and designs, we have got all the answers in this blog post about which type of doors best suit your needs.

By the time you finish this article you will know exactly what type of door you need and how to discuss your options with a reliable door manufacturer you can trust.

Steps for Choosing a Door

Choosing the right type of door for your home or business is an important decision, and one that shouldn't be taken lightly. The type of door you choose will depend on a variety of factors such as purpose, location, and climate, to name a few.

Having the right door can help to keep you safe and secure, while also offering aesthetic value to your living or working space. It can also save you money in the long run if it is properly maintained and kept in good condition. Knowing what kind of door will best suit your needs is essential if you want to make sure your investment lasts for many years to come.

Step 1: Know What Types of Doors are Available

There's a lot more to choosing the right type of door than meets the eye. From interior doors to exterior doors, to wooden doors, PVC doors, MDF doors, and veneer there are countless styles and materials available.

Interior door options include solid wood panel doors, hollow core flush doors, French doors with glass panes, pocket or sliding closet and wardrobe doors, bi-folding closet or room dividers and many more.

For exterior entryways you can choose from wood to veneer and PVC entry systems. And if you want something more robust, go with a custom made steel security door.

No matter what type of door you end up getting, make sure it fits both your lifestyle needs as well as the aesthetics of your living space!

Step 2: Know Your Door's Purpose

Everybody knows the basic purpose of a door, but it's a lot more than a simple device that opens and closes. Doors can be decorative, or they can keep residents and employees safe.

So, the second step in making the best selection for your door will be to consider it's purpose.

Different types of doors offer different levels of security and aesthetic appeal. For example, an interior door may not need to be as secure as an exterior one but should still look great. On the other hand, you will want to make sure that your front entrance is strong enough to provide safety and security.

Think about who will be using the door most often - you? Your family? Employees or customers? Each of these scenarios requires a different type of door with varying levels of insulation, noise reduction, ventilation, durability and more

If you have kids or pets around the house, you may want to opt for something sturdier than usual too! You may also be interested in choosing a door according to energy efficiency standards so you can save on energy bills.

Step 3: Consider Your Location and Climate

The location and climate of where you live should be taken into account when choosing a door.. Different materials react differently to extreme temperatures, so make sure to choose material that’s suitable for your region.

If you are in an area that gets a lot of snow, then an insulated fiberglass door would be a good choice as it is designed to stand up against strong winds and cold weathers.

On the other hand, steel doors would be more suitable for dryer climates because they are resistant to heat than other materials. Likewise, if you live in an area with high humidity levels consider a vinyl-clad door because of its water-resistant properties and ability to withstand constant moisture.

Step 4: Check Building Codes

Your local council is the authority on door safety, and they have codes and standards in place to keep your family and belongings safe. Make sure to check if your door needs to be fire safet, impact resistant, or windstorm rated. It's important to make sure your door is up to code, because local governments will force you to replace it at more expense to you.

Step 5: Pre-Hung or Door Slab

A door slab is simply a door with no frame, hardware, hinges, or handle - you will have to install it yourself. However, it's a bit more forgiving when it comes to imperfect installations. And you'll need to be sure to check the door thickness, which typically ranges from 1-3/8" - 2-1/4". On the other hand, pre hung doors come with the frame, hardware, and other features. This is typically the way to go when buying an exterior door, as they are prepped to be weather tight.

Step 6: Maintenance

Maintenance should always be a top consideration when selecting the right door for your home or business. Depending on the type of door you select, general maintenance will vary and some may require more upkeep than others.

For instance, if you go with a wooden door, it may need to be stained or treated more often to prevent rot and warping due to water damage. Whereas, a steel door will shrug off bad weather and rough treatment, but will still need regular cleaning and maintenance to ensure trouble-free operation over time.

No matter what type of door you select, make sure that you factor maintenance into your decision; otherwise, that great deal might end up costing much more down the line!
